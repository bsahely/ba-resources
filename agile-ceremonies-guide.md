# Agile Ceremonies Guide

*By Sahely Banerjee · [bsahely.github.io](https://bsahely.github.io)*

---

## The Ceremonies at a Glance

| Ceremony | When | Duration (2-week sprint) | Who |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sprint Planning | Start of sprint | 2–4 hours | Whole team |
| Daily Standup | Every day | 15 minutes | Dev team + BA/PO |
| Backlog Refinement | Mid-sprint | 1–2 hours | BA/PO + Dev leads |
| Sprint Review | End of sprint | 1 hour | Team + stakeholders |
| Retrospective | End of sprint | 1 hour | Team only |

---

## Sprint Planning

**Purpose:** Agree what the team will build this sprint and how.

**BA/PO responsibilities:**
- Top of backlog is refined, estimated, and ready
- Every story has acceptance criteria attached
- Priority order is clear and defended
- You can answer "why" for every story in the top 10

**What good looks like:**
- Team pulls stories confidently without needing clarification
- Commitment is realistic — not everything in the backlog
- Sprint goal is one clear sentence the whole team can repeat

**What bad looks like:**
- 🚩 Stories are estimated in the meeting for the first time
- 🚩 Team asks basic "what does this mean?" questions
- 🚩 No sprint goal — just a list of tickets
- 🚩 PO is absent

**Facilitation tip:** 
Use "what questions do you have?" not "does everyone understand?" 
The second question gets nodding. The first gets honesty.

## Daily Standup

**Purpose:** Surface blockers fast. Not a status report.

**The three questions:**
1. What did I complete yesterday?
2. What am I working on today?
3. Is anything blocking me?

**BA/PO role in standup:**
- Listen for blockers you can unblock (missing requirements, unclear AC)
- Note anything that puts the sprint goal at risk
- Don't run it — that's the Scrum Master's job

**What good looks like:**
- Done in 15 minutes or less
- Blockers are raised and someone takes ownership
- Side conversations happen after, not during

**What bad looks like:**
- 🚩 People report to the PO, not to each other
- 🚩 Problem-solving happens in the standup itself
- 🚩 "No blockers" every day — someone isn't being honest

## Backlog Refinement (Grooming)

**Purpose:** Prepare future sprint stories so planning is smooth.

**What happens:**
- BA walks through upcoming stories
- Team asks questions, BA answers or takes actions
- Stories get estimated (story points or T-shirt sizing)
- Stories that are too big get split

**BA/PO responsibilities:**
- Have stories 2 sprints ahead refined and ready
- Bring supporting context: wireframes, process maps, data samples
- Take notes on every question — they're often missing requirements

**Ready criteria — a story is ready for sprint when:**
- [ ] Written in agreed user story format
- [ ] Acceptance criteria attached
- [ ] Dependencies identified
- [ ] Estimated by the team
- [ ] PO has prioritised it

## Sprint Review (Demo)

**Purpose:** Show working software to stakeholders. Get feedback.

**BA/PO responsibilities:**
- Organise the demo — who shows what
- Invite the right stakeholders (not just sponsors — include end users)
- Capture feedback formally, not just verbally
- Celebrate what shipped

**What good looks like:**
- Real working software demoed, not slides
- Stakeholders give specific feedback
- PO makes live prioritisation calls based on feedback

**What bad looks like:**
- 🚩 Demo of a PowerPoint instead of the actual product
- 🚩 No stakeholders — just the team talking to themselves
- 🚩 Feedback is ignored or not captured

## Retrospective

**Purpose:** Improve how the team works. Psychological safety required.

**The classic format:**
- **Went well** — keep doing this
- **Didn't go well** — stop or change this
- **Try next sprint** — one or two concrete experiments

**BA/PO role:**
- Participate as a full team member — you're not above this
- Acknowledge your own contribution to problems
- Follow through on actions you own

**Facilitation formats to rotate:**
- **Start / Stop / Continue** — simple and fast
- **4Ls** — Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For
- **Sailboat** — Wind (helps), Anchors (slows), Rocks (risks), Island (goal)

**What good looks like:**
- Real problems are named, not just positivity
- Actions are specific with an owner and a deadline
- Last retro's actions are reviewed first

**What bad looks like:**
- 🚩 PO or lead is defensive when problems are raised
- 🚩 Same issues appear every retro with no resolution
- 🚩 Actions like "communicate better" — too vague to act on
